WHAT IS THE SEP PROGRAM?

The Software Engineering Pilot (SEP) is a computer science and software engineering class for students from grades 6 to 12. The program curriculum varies from computer programming, robotics, web design, mobile development, physical computing and SO MUCH MORE.
It allows for students to develop problem-solving skills, collaborate with others, build up leadership skills, prepare for the outside world and show off their creativity.

Opportunities


Field Trips

SEP students go on field trips in order to learn more about programming languages and meet tech-related companies.

Hackathons

SEP students travel to different events, one being a hackathon, which allows students to develop useful applications.

Internships

SEP students will take part in internships, where they get hands-on experience learning more about code, robotics, and etc.

Year-end Showcases

SEP students have the opportunity to present their showcases, where students show off their work at the end of the year.

Events

SEP students will be involved in a variety of events while working in a group and meeting new people.

Summer Programs

SEP students will have chances to get extra practice for previously learned programming languages.
